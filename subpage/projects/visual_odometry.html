Visual Odometry for Indoor Localization
Visual Odometry for Indoor Localization
Jun. 2016 - Aug. 2016
Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University

Supervisor: Howie Choset
We propose a system of combining the pose from LOAM (LIDAR odometry and mapping) and visual feature points (ORB) to build an initial map of the environment, then localize a mobile robot with only a monocular camera onboard and build a denser map based on the initial map to enhance accuracy compared with pure monocular visual odometry. In this way, the localization accuracy of a mobile robot in indoor environment could be significantly improved with a single LIDAR, of which the one- time cost is ignorable for a large amount of robots and for a long term.

Also built the multi-camera visual odometry environment in Gazebo, and tested the system on Snake Monster and C-Snake.
